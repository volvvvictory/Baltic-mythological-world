# Baltic Mythological World

An interactive 3D exploration of Baltic cosmology built with [Three.js](https://threejs.org/). The scene is a single self-contained HTML file.

## What it is

The world is structured around the **World Tree** (*Pasaulio medis*), the axis connecting three realms of Baltic belief:

| Realm | Baltic name | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Sky World | *Dangus* | The canopy — home of celestial bodies and sky deities |
| Middle World | *Žemė* | The living earth — forests, lake, hillfort village |
| Underworld | *Požemis* | The roots — realm of the dead and Velnias |

## Features

- **Three-realm cosmology** — orbit or walk through each world via the navigation buttons
- **Nine celestial bodies** suspended from the crown branches: Saule (Sun), Meness (Moon), Auseklis (Morning Star), and six planetary deities — hover to see their names
- **Hillfort village** on the Middle World hill with palisade fence, entrance gate, longhouses, tents, and campfire
- **Walk mode** — enter first-person exploration from the south path entrance
- **Lore panel** — click glowing objects to read mythological context
- **Lake** in the north-east quadrant with lily pads, reeds, and a docked ship
- **Monument Valley-style roots** intertwining beneath the trunk into the Underworld
- **Low-poly flat-shaded aesthetic** throughout

## Mythological references

Baltic (Lithuanian and Latvian) mythology centres on the World Tree as the cosmic axis. Key deities represented:

- **Saule** — Sun goddess, weaves light and governs fate
- **Meness** — Moon god, marks time and guards the dead
- **Auseklis** — Morning Star, herald of dawn
- **Perkons / Perkūnas** — Thunder god, enemy of Velnias
- **Velnias / Vels** — Underworld deity, guardian of cattle and the dead
- **Laima** — goddess of fate and luck
- **Dievas** — sky god, overseer of cosmic order
